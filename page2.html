Overview of J.R.R. Tolkien

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien did many other things in life other than writing the saga he is critically acclaimed for. He was a professor at Oxford University and "taught Old English language and literature" from the period in England between 450 and 1150 C.E. (Clair 63). While teaching at Oxford, he founded a club to encourage the reading and discussion of Icelandic stories (qtd. Carpenter 119-120). Because of his love for Nordic, specifically Icelandic lore, "Hobbit share many characteristics of the Icelanders of the famous family sagas" (Clair 64). Prior to being a professor, he served during World War 1, as well as World War II as a code breaker. It was his time fighting in World War I that inspired him to create the land of Mordor: a hellscape of war-centered industrialism.
